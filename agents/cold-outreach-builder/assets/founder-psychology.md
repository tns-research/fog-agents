# Founder Psychology (cold outreach)

How founders read, react to, and reply to cold messages. Used by `copywriting-en/SKILL.md`, `copywriting-fr/SKILL.md`, and `designing-sequences/SKILL.md` to choose angle by stage.

A founder ICP has very different reflexes from a corporate buyer. Treating a founder like a marketing-director persona is the most common mistake in B2B cold outreach to founders.

---

## Core psychology

| Trait | What it means in cold copy |
|-------|----------------------------|
| Time-poor | Body >120 words is unread. Subject decides the open. |
| Skeptical of marketing | Every claim must reference a verifiable artifact. "Trusted by 1000+" reads as filler. |
| Pattern-matchers | Spot AI-tells, template language, and copy-paste personalization in 2 seconds. |
| Peer-respectful | Respond to peer-to-peer tone (Unity, shared struggle). React badly to vendor-to-buyer tone. |
| Numerate | Concrete numbers always beat adjectives. "0 to 50 customers in 3 months" > "great results". |
| Outcome-anchored | Care about results, not features. Skip the "platform" / "ecosystem" framing. |
| Trust-economical | Will reply to one sender they trust over ten louder pitches. Liking and Authority compound. |

---

## Stage segmentation

A founder's psychology shifts as the company progresses. Same offer, different angle per stage.

| Stage | Profile | Pain | Best angle | Avoid |
|-------|---------|------|------------|-------|
| **Idea** | Pre-product, validating | Wrong problem, wrong ICP, no proof | Resource Share, frameworks for clarity | Anything implying "your business", there is none yet |
| **Proto** | Building MVP | Time pressure, technical debt, no users | BAB (Before / After / Bridge), free audit | Long-term ROI claims |
| **MVP** | Live product, no traction | First 10 customers, channel choice, message-market fit | Resource Share + first-customer playbook | Scaling-up topics |
| **20+ customers** | Early traction, pre-PMF | Repeatability, churn, channel saturation | Challenge Status Quo, peer benchmark | Treating them like a Series A |
| **Scale** | Post-PMF, team building | Hiring, ops, brand, fundraising | PPP, Authority, social proof from peers at their scale | "First customers" framing, irrelevant at this stage |

## Angle by stage (worked phrasings)

### Idea stage
- Hook: "Most founders pick the wrong ICP twice before the third sticks."
- CTA: "Want a 5-minute checklist on validating ICP before you build?"
- Lever: Reciprocity.

### Proto stage
- Hook: "{{first_name}}, we have helped 50+ teams ship the right MVP in under 6 weeks."
- CTA: "If you are MVP-stage, want a free 30-min audit on what to cut?"
- Lever: Reciprocity + Liking (shared struggle).

### MVP stage
- Hook: "0 to 10 customers is the hardest stretch. Most founders confuse channel saturation with bad message."
- CTA: "Free 12-point checklist on the first-10-customers gap. Send it?"
- Lever: Reciprocity + Authority.

### 20+ stage
- Hook: "30 SaaS at the same activation gap. Same root cause: ICP drift around month 4."
- CTA: "Worth comparing notes on activation? 15 minutes."
- Lever: Social proof + Authority.

### Scale stage
- Hook: "Saw your team's hire announcements last month. The ops layer is usually the next bottleneck after PMF."
- CTA: "If ops scaling is on the roadmap, we have built for that exact transition. 20 minutes Thursday?"
- Lever: Authority + Liking (specific observation).

## Anti-corporate reflexes

Founders react badly to:

- "Solutions provider" / "we provide solutions". Use a verb instead.
- "Cutting-edge platform" / "best-in-class". Use the concrete tool name.
- "We help X do Y faster". Vague. Use the actual outcome.
- Logo-walls. Founders trust one named peer over twenty logos.
- "Schedule a demo". Founders book a 15-minute exchange, not a demo.
- "Decision-maker". Founders are decision-makers. Do not flatter them with a label they own.

---

## Real proof beats claimed credibility

Founders verify. Always assume the recipient will:

- Click your domain.
- Read your About page.
- Look at your team's LinkedIn profiles.
- Search your product or company name.

If any of those look weak (no domain, no public team, no traceable customer), the cold message lands as spam regardless of the copy.

**Rule.** Before sending, audit your own digital footprint. The cold message is one part of the message. The sender's verifiability is the other.

---

## Shared struggle (Unity activation)

The "we have been there" angle is among the highest-converting framings *if* the sender has actually been there. Worked patterns:

- "Working solo on acquisition is exhausting; I did the same in 2024 and..."
- "Most founders I talk to spent 3 months on the wrong channel before fixing it; we did 4."
- "We started where you are now. The thing we wish we had known..."

If the sender has not been there, do not fake it. Switch to Reciprocity (give a useful resource) or Authority (cite specific data).

## Cadence and tolerance

| Behaviour | Founder reaction |
|-----------|------------------|
| 6 messages, widening gaps, last is breakup | Tolerated, sometimes appreciated |
| 3 messages in 4 days | Annoying, often blocks the sender |
| Same message rephrased 4 times | Burns trust permanently |
| One useful resource shared in Email 3 | Often the trigger for a reply |
| Personalization that resolves to "your work" | Read as bulk send, ignored or marked spam |

The widening-gap cadence (Day 0, +3, +7, +12, +18, +26) respects time-poor founders better than tight cadences.

---

## Notes

These psychology rules are calibrated for founders specifically. For non-founder ICPs (PMs, RevOps, Heads of, marketing leads), the angle shifts: more peer-language than founder-language, more Authority than Unity. The skill files explicitly call out when an angle is founder-only.
