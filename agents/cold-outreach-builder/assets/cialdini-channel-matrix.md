# Cialdini × Channel Matrix

Lookup table for `applying-cialdini-to-cold-email/SKILL.md`. For each of Cialdini's 7 principles, the top tactic per channel and the failure mode that destroys it.

The skill explains how to use this table; this asset is the data.

---

## The matrix

| # | Principle | Cold email tactic | LinkedIn DM tactic | Failure mode |
|---|-----------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------|
| 1 | Reciprocity | Lead with a specific insight or mini-audit attached. Resource-share message: name the artifact, name its concrete value, attach or offer to send. | Voice note with a specific tip relevant to the prospect's recent post. Or a Loom answering a question they asked publicly. | "Free guide" with a marketing landing page behind it. Reciprocity breaks when the gift requires a form. |
| 2 | Commitment | Email 1 CTA is a micro-yes ("worth 10 min next Thursday?"), not "book a 30-min call". Follow-up references the prior implicit commitment. | First DM after accept asks one specific yes/no question. Follow-up references the prior message naturally. | Faking a prior commitment ("as we discussed last week"). Detected immediately. |
| 3 | Social proof | Name one comparable peer customer with one concrete outcome metric. "0 to 50 clients in 3 months" beats "great results". | Name a mutual connection with consent, or a mutual community ("from the X cohort"). Avoid logo-wall. | "Trusted by 1000+ companies" without specifics. Reads as marketing. |
| 4 | Authority | One credibility sentence woven into the hook or body, not a bio. Reference a publication, talk, dataset, or named outcome they can verify. | Authority is implicit on LinkedIn (your profile shows it). Do not repeat your title in the DM. Reference one talk or article instead. | A bio paragraph claiming authority. Authority claimed = authority lost. |
| 5 | Liking | Shared struggle: show you have been where they are with one specific moment, not "as a founder myself". Sincere observation tied to a real public artifact. | Comment-then-DM is the highest-liking tactic on LinkedIn. The comment proves you read their work. | Generic flattery ("I admire your work"). Detected as template. |
| 6 | Scarcity | Honest scarcity only: real cohort cap, real event seat limit, real beta headcount. Frame as what they would miss. | Same as email. LinkedIn audiences are MORE skeptical of urgency, not less. | Manufactured urgency (countdown timers, "last chance" when it isn't). Permanent credibility loss. |
| 7 | Unity | Write peer-to-peer when both sender and recipient share a real category. The category does not need to be named. "Working solo on acquisition is hard" activates Unity if both are solo founders. | Reference a shared community / cohort / ex-employer / alumni group when real. The shared category does the work. | Forced "we founders all know..." when sender is not actually one of them. |

## Per-message lever assignment heuristic

| Message purpose | Default lever | Alternative |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------|
| Initial hook | Reciprocity OR Liking | Authority (one sentence) |
| Bump | Commitment | Social proof |
| Value drop | Reciprocity | Authority |
| Social proof | Social proof | Unity |
| Direct ask | Commitment | Scarcity (only if real) |
| Breakup | Liking OR Unity | Reciprocity |

---

## Persuasion audit checklist

Run before shipping any sequence:

- [ ] Each message names exactly one Cialdini lever.
- [ ] The lever named is actually present in the body, not only labelled.
- [ ] At least 4 distinct levers across a 6-message sequence.
- [ ] No single lever stacked in 3 consecutive messages.
- [ ] No manufactured urgency anywhere.
- [ ] No claimed authority (titles, "expert", "thought leader") without verifiable proof.
- [ ] Social proof, when present, is specific (peer name, vertical, metric).
- [ ] Reciprocity, when present, is unconditional (no form gating the gift).
- [ ] Liking is grounded in a real artifact (post, hire, talk, raise), not a generic compliment.
- [ ] Unity, when present, names a category both sender and recipient actually share.

A sequence that fails 3+ items is not ready. Rewrite the offending messages.

---

## Notes

The mapping is a heuristic, not a law. A B2B founder ICP might respond better to Authority + Reciprocity in Email 1 than to Liking. The default lever per message is a starting point; let the ICP scan inform the actual choice.

Verbatim quotes used as social proof should preserve original wording (cross-cutting convention C5). When the source is FR and the message is EN (or vice versa), translate the quote and note `[translated]`, never paraphrase silently.
