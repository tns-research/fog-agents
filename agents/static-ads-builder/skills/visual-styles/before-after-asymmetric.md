# Before/After Asymmetric

## Description

Two-panel split composition where the "before" state (problem) takes 35% of the frame and the "after" state (resolution, with product) takes 65%. The visual imbalance is intentional: by giving the resolution more frame real estate, the eye is directed toward the outcome rather than dwelling on the problem. The asymmetry also makes the format feel editorial rather than the symmetrical "before/after" grid that has become a cliché in the wellness category.

## Performance signal

Strongest in categories with visible transformation: beauty, wellness, food, home. The 27% carousel outperformance for transformation content (Meta 2025 data) translates to single-frame statics when the asymmetric weighting is applied, the lopsided composition creates visual tension that a 50/50 split does not. The format reads as evidence of change, not just a claim.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Avoid. The before/after format only lands when the audience recognizes both states as personally relevant, which requires prior awareness of the problem. On cold audiences, the "before" state reads as random, not relatable.
- **MOFU:** Primary fit. Warm audiences comparing options need to see the transformation as a proof-of-concept. This format shows both states as real, not illustrated.
- **BOFU (light):** Valid when the "after" state includes the product + offer framing. The right panel becomes the conversion visual; the left panel is the reminder of why they're here.

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed). 9:16 for stories. Square 1:1 works but the 35/65 split is more compressed.
- **Camera position:** Agent selects for both panels, typically eye level, tight framing on the relevant subject (skin, product in context, environment). Both panels use the same camera height and angle for visual coherence.
- **Focal length:** 85mm or 100mm for close-up subject panels (skin, face, product detail). 50mm for wider context panels.
- **Lighting:**
  - **Left panel (before/35%):** Flatter, cooler, slightly underlit, conveys the "problem" state without being dramatic. Overcast or diffuse overhead; no warmth.
  - **Right panel (after/65%):** Warmer, more directional, aspirational, butterfly or soft key light. Visually richer and more inviting than the left.
- **Color grade:**
  - **Left panel:** Cool-shifted, muted, slightly desaturated.
  - **Right panel:** Warm, slightly lifted, more saturated.
  - The grade split reinforces the narrative direction without the panels needing text labels.
- **Environment:** Agent selects, the same environment should appear in both panels for visual coherence (same background context, same surface). The lighting and grade carry the emotional distinction.
- **Composition logic:**
  - Left panel: 35% of frame width. Problem state. Subject slightly smaller, lighting flatter. No product (the "without" state).
  - Right panel: 65% of frame width. Resolution state. Product visible. Lighting warmer and more directional. Subject or scene appears noticeably better.
  - A thin vertical dividing element (hard cut, thin line, or natural transition) separates the two panels.
  - No text labels ("Before" / "After"), the visual contrast should carry the narrative without labels, which read as low-effort.
  - Reserve bottom strip (full width) for headline and subheadline.
- **Subject:** Agent selects, can be a face/skin close-up, a product in context, or an environment. Both panels must show the same subject at the same framing.
- **Exclusions:** No text labels "Before" or "After" in the frame. No extreme retouching on either panel, both must look real. No symmetrical 50/50 split, the asymmetry is structural to this style.

## Typography notes

- **Headline placement:** Bottom strip spanning full frame width, visible across both panels. Single background (dark overlay or the natural lower area of the composition).
- **Font style:** Agent selects, should be legible across the full-width bottom strip without competing with either panel.
- **No "Before / After" labels in the visual.** The grade split and composition are the narrative; labels are redundant and lower the perceived quality.
- **Subheadline:** Directly below headline in the same bottom strip.

## Constraints

- **No symmetrical 50/50 split.** The 35/65 weighting is structural, it is what makes this format editorial rather than generic. A 50/50 split becomes a standard comparison graphic.
- Both panels must **look real**: no extreme smoothing or retouching on the after panel; no extreme degradation on the before panel. Audiences reject obvious manipulation.
- **No text labels** ("Before", "Après", "Without", "With") in frame.
- The product must appear **in the right (after) panel only**: placing it in the before panel breaks the narrative logic.

## Example use

MOFU brief (False Choice Collapse angle) for a skincare brand: left panel (35%) shows a woman's cheek area under flat cool light, natural skin. Right panel (65%) shows the same framing, warmer butterfly light, skin appearing rested and luminous, the CO₂ mask product visible lower-right. Bottom strip headline: "Pas besoin de choisir entre une piqûre et votre miroir."
