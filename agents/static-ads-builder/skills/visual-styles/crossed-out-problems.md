# Crossed-Out Problems

## Description

A typography-dominant static that lists 3-5 common problems or pain points, each with a bold strikethrough line crossing them out. The product appears below or beside the list as the implicit resolution. The crossed-out text creates a visual satisfaction signal, the problems are eliminated, and the product is positioned as the reason why. No explanation needed; the visual logic does the work.

## Performance signal

"Seeing problems crossed out is super satisfying, like checking everything off a to-do list" (Magritte/20K-ad analysis). Used by Jones Road, Seed, and Heights at scale on Meta. The format works because it activates the brain's completion bias: once you see items being crossed off, you want to see them all resolved. The product at the bottom completes the visual narrative.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Avoid. The problems only resonate if the audience already recognizes them as personal pain points, which requires category awareness.
- **MOFU:** Primary fit. The audience knows their problem, this format validates it and positions the product as the fix.
- **BOFU:** Valid as a reminder/conversion nudge. The crossed-out problems remind the audience why they were considering the product in the first place.

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed). 9:16 for Stories (more vertical space for the list).
- **Camera position:** Frontal, clean, minimal. Product is a secondary visual element, the text is primary.
- **Focal length:** 50-85mm for product. Sharp, clean.
- **Lighting:** Even, bright, studio softbox or flat light. No drama, the drama is in the text.
- **Color grade:** Clean, bright. Background in a single solid color (white, cream, or light brand color). Strikethrough lines in a contrasting color (red, dark grey, or brand accent).
- **Environment:** Minimal. Solid-color background or subtle texture. Nothing competes with the text list.
- **Composition logic:**
  - **Upper 60-70%:** 3-5 problem statements stacked vertically, each with a bold strikethrough line drawn through the text. The strikethrough should look decisive, not a thin line, but a thick, confident stroke (hand-drawn style or bold geometric line).
  - **Lower 30-40%:** Product standing upright, label visible, cleanly placed. The product is the visual resolution, it sits below the eliminated problems.
  - **Optional:** A small transition element between the list and the product, an arrow, a line, or simply the product headline.
  - The strikethrough color should contrast with the text color for maximum visual impact.
- **Subject:** No human subject required. Text-and-product forward.
- **Exclusions:** No lifestyle imagery. No more than 5 problems (list fatigue). No small or thin strikethroughs, they must be visible at mobile thumbnail scale. No explanatory text for each problem, the problem statement alone must be self-evident.

## Typography notes

- **Problem text** is the dominant visual element. Bold sans-serif, large enough to read at thumbnail. Each problem is one short phrase (3-8 words).
- **Strikethrough** should be thick, visible, and slightly hand-drawn in feel, not a CSS text-decoration. It should feel decisive, like someone took a marker and crossed it out.
- **Headline** (if present) sits above the problem list or integrates with it. It frames the narrative: "Plus jamais ça." or the product name as the resolution.
- **Subheadline** (if present) sits near the product, below the list. It confirms the product as the solution.
- **Font style:** Bold geometric sans-serif for problems. Same font for headline but heavier weight.

## Constraints

- **3-5 problems only.** Fewer feels incomplete; more creates list fatigue and tiny text.
- **Each problem is 3-8 words.** Not sentences, compressed pain point statements. "Peau qui tire", "Rougeurs après la douche", "Gels qui collent".
- **The product must appear in the frame.** The format requires the product as the visual resolution, without it, the problems are just a list of complaints.
- **No explanatory sub-text per problem.** Each strikethrough item is self-evident. Explanations kill the visual rhythm.
- **Strikethroughs must be visible at thumbnail scale.** Test mental thumbnail: if you can't see the lines crossing out text, they're too thin.
