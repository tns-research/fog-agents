# Us vs. Them Comparison

## Description

A two-column static that places the product against a generic or competitor alternative. The left column shows the inferior option with X marks or muted presentation; the right column shows the product with checkmarks, brighter treatment, and winning features. The visual imbalance, dull left vs. vibrant right, makes the choice feel obvious without the viewer needing to think.

## Performance signal

"Humans have a natural tendency to compare things. It's not a fair fight, and that's the point" (Backlinko, Manukora example). Comparison statics are top performers in supplements and skincare on Meta (Curtis Howland, $100M+ spend). The format works because it collapses a complex buying decision into a binary visual: old way bad, new way good. Brands like Manukora, Grüns, and Heights run this consistently.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Light use only. Cold audiences need to care about the category before a comparison lands. Use only when the "them" is a universally recognized inferior alternative (e.g. generic supermarket gels vs. your premium product).
- **MOFU:** Primary and ideal fit. The audience is comparing options. This format makes the comparison for them, removing effort and guiding the decision.
- **BOFU:** Avoid. At BOFU the comparison is already settled, they need an offer or friction reduction, not more comparison.

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed). 9:16 for Stories. Square 1:1 works but columns are tighter.
- **Camera position:** Frontal, clean, analytical. Both columns at same angle for fair visual comparison.
- **Focal length:** 50-85mm equivalent. Clean product rendering.
- **Lighting:**
  - **Left column (them):** Slightly flatter, cooler, muted, conveys "generic" without being dramatic.
  - **Right column (us):** Warmer, brighter, more saturated, conveys "premium" and "better choice."
- **Color grade:**
  - **Left column:** Desaturated, grey-shifted, muted. Red X marks or dimmed icons.
  - **Right column:** Saturated, warm, brand-color accents. Green checkmarks or brand-color icons.
- **Environment:** Clean background. Left column: plain grey or muted neutral. Right column: clean white, cream, or brand primary tint.
- **Composition logic:**
  - **Two vertical columns**, roughly 50/50 width split (or 45/55 favoring the product side).
  - **Left column header:** "Gels classiques" / "Them" / "Other [category]", in muted text.
  - **Right column header:** "[Brand]" / "Us" / "[Product name]", in bold brand-color text.
  - **Under each header:** Product image (left: generic/unbranded; right: actual product).
  - **Below products:** 3-5 feature lines. Left: each preceded by ✗ or X icon in red/grey. Right: each preceded by ✓ or checkmark in green/brand color.
  - **Feature lines must mirror each other**: same category compared side by side (e.g., "10% aloé vera" vs. "94% aloé vera").
  - Thin vertical divider between columns (line, space, or color boundary).
- **Subject:** No human subject. Product-and-text forward.
- **Exclusions:** No named competitor brands (use generic category instead). No more than 5 comparison lines. No lifestyle imagery. No complex background.

## Typography notes

- **Headline** spans both columns at top. Bold, large. States the comparison frame: "Ce que vous utilisez vs. ce que votre peau mérite."
- **Column headers** are secondary, smaller, but clearly differentiate the two sides.
- **Feature lines** are rendered inside the image. Same font size on both sides. Left in muted grey/dark; right in bold dark or brand color.
- **Checkmarks and X marks** are color-coded and visually distinct. Same size, aligned vertically.
- **Font style:** Geometric sans-serif throughout. Bold for headers, medium for feature lines.

## Constraints

- **Never name a specific competitor brand.** Use generic category terms ("gels classiques", "crèmes en supermarché", "l'alternative standard"). Naming competitors creates legal risk and looks petty.
- **Feature lines must be factual and verifiable.** Do not invent claims.
- **Mirror structure is mandatory**: each feature on the left must have a corresponding feature on the right. Asymmetric lists break the comparison logic.
- **3-5 feature lines maximum.** More creates clutter; fewer feels incomplete.
- **MOFU only as primary.** Do not use as the lead format for cold TOFU audiences (they don't know enough to compare yet).

## Example use

MOFU brief for NaturAloé Gel Originel: Left column "Gels d'aloé vera classiques" with generic tube, X marks: "Reconstitué à partir de poudre", "10-20% d'aloé vera", "Additifs et parfums", "Provenance inconnue". Right column "NaturAloé" with product, checkmarks: "Gel natif pressé à froid", "94% d'aloé vera pur", "Sans parfum, certifié Bio", "Cultivé en France depuis 2002". Headline: "Tous les gels d'aloé vera ne se valent pas."
