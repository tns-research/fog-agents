# Ingredient/Origin Strip

## Description

A single static frame with a split composition: raw source ingredients, origin terrain, or a process detail on one half; the finished product cleanly placed on the other half. Communicates "what's inside" and "why it's better" without requiring the audience to read a word. The visual juxtaposition does the educational work, the origin half earns the product half.

## Performance signal

Carousels using transformation and ingredient breakdown outperformed single-image ads by 27% in 2025 (Meta DTC data). The single-frame static adaptation applies the same principle: the left-to-right (or top-to-bottom) visual logic of origin → product creates an implicit "from this, we made this" narrative that the audience reads without effort. Strongest in categories where provenance, formulation, or sourcing is a differentiator, food, skincare, supplements, beverage.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Avoid. The format requires the audience to care about the source, which assumes prior interest in the product or category. On cold audiences, a split composition of "raw ingredient + product" needs too much context to land.
- **MOFU:** Primary and exclusive fit. The audience is comparing options and wants to know why this product is better. This format answers that question visually, not verbally. "You can see where it comes from" is one of the most powerful consideration-stage proof signals.
- **BOFU:** Avoid as primary. The educational framing is wrong for conversion, BOFU needs friction reduction, not education. Use it only as a secondary element (e.g. small origin detail in background of a product hero).

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed). 9:16 for stories. Square 1:1 works but the split is tighter.
- **Camera position:** Agent selects, typically slightly elevated (30°), frontal, to give both halves clean flat visibility. Avoid worm's eye or profile angles that distort the split.
- **Focal length:**
  - **Origin half:** 35-50mm equivalent for environmental context or 100mm macro for ingredient close-up detail.
  - **Product half:** 85mm equivalent for clean product hero with background separation.
- **Lighting:** Even lighting across both halves for visual coherence, so the split reads as intentional editorial, not as two unrelated photos stitched together. If a mood difference is needed: origin half slightly warmer/earthier, product half cleaner/brighter.
- **Color grade:** Cohesive across both halves. A slight warm-neutral grade that works for both organic material (origin) and a packshot (product). No extreme contrast between the two halves in grade.
- **Environment:**
  - **Origin half:** Raw material in context, ingredient close-up (texture, grain, liquid), origin landscape (farm terrace, production environment), or process detail. Real and specific, not stock-looking.
  - **Product half:** Clean placement on a neutral surface. Product is the primary focus; background is simple and non-competing.
- **Composition logic:**
  - Left half (or top half): origin, ingredient, or process. Rich in texture and detail. Slightly organic, imperfect.
  - Right half (or bottom half): product cleanly placed. Label visible and clear.
  - A thin vertical dividing element or natural visual transition separates the two halves. The transition can be a hard line, a soft gradient edge, or a physical overlap where a detail from the left bleeds across.
  - Headline confirms the link between the two halves, it names the connection the visual implies.
- **Subject:** No human subject required. The visual is object-forward.
- **Exclusions:** No marble surface as the only background on the product side. No generic stock photography for the origin side, it must feel specific and real. No heavy text in the origin half competing with the product in the right.

## Typography notes

- **Headline confirms the link:** It should name the connection between origin and product, e.g. "De la source au soin." or "Le CO₂ pénètre là où les crèmes s'arrêtent." The headline doesn't need to explain; the visual already shows. It confirms.
- **Placement:** Upper strip or lower strip across the full frame width, not competing with either half.
- **Font style:** Clean, editorial, serif or light sans-serif. Not bold or heavy; the visual is doing the work.

## Constraints

- The origin half must be **specific and real**: a generic "nature" image breaks the educational narrative. It must be visually connected to the actual product ingredient or process.
- **No human subject required.** The style is object and material-forward.
- **MOFU only.** Do not use at TOFU (requires context) or BOFU (wrong register for conversion).
- The two halves must feel like **parts of the same creative**, not two images stitched together, consistent lighting and grade are essential.

## Example use

MOFU brief (Process Reveal angle) for a skincare brand: left half shows a macro close-up of CO₂ activation on skin texture, fine bubbles, real skin grain; right half shows the Nightn'go CO₂ mask product standing cleanly on a warm linen surface. Thin vertical line dividing them. Upper strip headline: "La plupart des masques hydratent. Le nôtre oxygène."
