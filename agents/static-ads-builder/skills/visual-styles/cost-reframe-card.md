# Cost Reframe Card

## Description

A typography-forward or product + text creative that reframes the product's price against an anchor the audience already accepts without hesitation, a coffee shop visit, a streaming subscription, a single clinic session. The math is simple and immediate: the product costs less than what they already spend on [X]. The headline does the calculation; the product confirms what they get.

## Performance signal

Consistently effective at BOFU for subscription and repeatable-purchase products (Meta DTC 2025 data). The price objection is the most common final barrier for audiences who have already considered a product and nearly converted. Relative pricing removes the "too expensive" feeling by shifting the reference point from absolute price to a comparison the audience has already accepted. Works especially well when the anchor is something the audience spends on regularly without thinking.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Do not use. A price comparison requires prior product awareness, cold audiences have no context for why the price is worth comparing. Leading with price at TOFU signals low confidence in the product itself.
- **MOFU:** Avoid as primary. Light use acceptable if the comparison is highly striking (e.g. 10× cheaper than the clinical alternative). But MOFU audiences still need to be convinced the product works; a price comparison before that trust is built feels premature.
- **BOFU:** Exclusive primary fit. The audience has already considered the product. Price is their last objection. This format removes it.

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed). 9:16 for stories.
- **Camera position:** Agent selects, typically frontal or slightly elevated (30°), clean and analytical. The composition is typography-led; the product is present but secondary to the math.
- **Focal length:** 50-85mm for product. Sharp and clean.
- **Lighting:** Studio overhead softbox, cool-to-neutral white balance (5500-6000K), perfectly even and flat. No dramatic shadows. Clinical, precise, analytical, this is a rational argument, not an emotional one.
- **Color grade:** Clean, bright, neutral digital. No grain, no lifted blacks, no cinematic grade. The visual must be easy to read, not atmospheric.
- **Environment:** Minimal or clean, neutral background, no busy context. The attention belongs on the numbers and the product. A clean cream, off-white, or brand primary color background.
- **Composition logic:**
  - **Option A (typography-dominant):** Upper two-thirds: clean background with bold price comparison text ("Anchor price → Product price"). Lower third: product standing upright on the background. Headline and subheadline carry the math and the qualifier.
  - **Option B (product-left / math-right):** Product occupies left third, standing upright. Right two-thirds: large price comparison text. Headline anchors the comparison.
  - The math must be **immediately legible**: the audience should understand the comparison in under 2 seconds.
- **Subject:** No human subject required. Product-forward.
- **Exclusions:** No lifestyle imagery competing with the math. No moody or dark lighting. No complex compositions. The format is intentionally simple, complexity kills the "obvious value" feeling.

## Typography notes

- **Price comparison text** is the dominant visual element. Render it as extra-bold or black weight, large enough to read as a thumbnail.
- **Anchor price first** (what the audience already pays or would pay elsewhere), **product price second**: in a visually subordinate but clearly legible weight. The juxtaposition is the argument.
- **No decorative math symbols**: no flashy "vs." graphics. The numbers should speak plainly.
- **Headline** carries the framing: "Une séance CO₂ en clinique : à partir de 150 €.", `subheadline` delivers the resolution: "Notre masque : 64 €. Chez vous, quand vous voulez."

## Constraints

- **BOFU only.** Do not use at TOFU (no prior awareness) or early MOFU (trust not yet established).
- The anchor must be **accurate and verifiable**: do not invent comparison prices. Only use prices confirmed in consumer research, competitor analysis, or brand context.
- The format must remain **visually simple**: no complex compositions competing with the price argument.
- Do not use **percentage-based comparisons** ("67% cheaper"), they feel manipulative. Use absolute prices for authenticity.

## Example use

BOFU brief (Cost Reframe angle) for a skincare brand: clean cream background, bold dark text upper two-thirds: "Une séance CO₂ en clinique : à partir de 150 €." / "Notre masque : 64 €. Chez vous, quand vous voulez." CO₂ mask product standing upright in the lower third, label facing camera. Analytical, no props, no drama.
