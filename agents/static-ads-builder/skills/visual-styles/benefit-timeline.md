# Benefit Timeline

## Description

A vertical static that organizes product benefits along a time axis, Day 1, Week 1, Month 1 (or similar progression). The product appears at the top or side; the timeline flows downward showing cumulative results over time. The format is uniquely persuasive because it plants the seed for consistent use, the viewer doesn't just see what the product does, they see what it does over time. This transforms a single purchase into a ritual.

## Performance signal

"Instead of using traditional benefit callouts or a normal before/after layout, the benefits are organized along a timeline. This is brilliant because it plants a seed for customers to use the product consistently in order to experience the benefits" (Backlinko, Everyday Dose example). The format maps directly to the Meta performance principle of "Results-first" hooks (Curtis Howland, 12 hook types), leading with transformation creates engagement. For consumable/repeatable-use products (skincare, supplements, food), the timeline is one of the strongest consideration-stage formats.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Avoid. Cold audiences need category awareness before a usage timeline feels relevant.
- **MOFU:** Primary fit. The timeline answers "what will happen if I commit to this?", the key consideration-stage question for repeatable-use products.
- **BOFU:** Strong fit. For warm audiences hesitating on purchase, the timeline provides a concrete vision of the payoff, it makes the investment feel tangible and worth it.

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed), ideal for vertical timeline flow. 9:16 for Stories, even more vertical space. Avoid 1:1, the timeline needs height.
- **Camera position:** Frontal, clean for product element. The layout is graphic-design-driven.
- **Focal length:** 50-85mm for product.
- **Lighting:** Even, bright for product element. Clean studio.
- **Color grade:** Clean, warm-neutral. Progressive warmth or saturation along the timeline (earliest stage slightly cooler, latest stage warmest) reinforces the improvement narrative.
- **Environment:** Clean background. No environmental context, the timeline structure IS the visual.
- **Composition logic:**
  - **Product at top or left**: hero placement, label visible. Anchors the timeline to the product.
  - **Timeline flows vertically downward** with 3-4 time markers, each containing:
    - A time label: "Jour 1", "Semaine 1", "Mois 1", "Mois 3" (or similar progression appropriate to the product).
    - A short benefit statement (3-8 words) describing what happens at that stage.
    - Optional: a small icon or color indicator that progresses (e.g., increasingly saturated green circles, or a filling bar).
  - **Visual progression:** The timeline should feel like it's building toward something. Each stage is slightly more vibrant, warmer, or visually "fuller" than the previous one.
  - **Connector line or dots** linking the time markers, a thin vertical line, dots, or dashes that visually connect the progression.
  - Headline at top framing the timeline: "Ce qui se passe quand vous l'utilisez chaque jour."
- **Subject:** No human subject required. Product-and-timeline forward. Optional: small before/after skin close-ups at first and last timeline stages.
- **Exclusions:** No more than 4 time stages (beyond that, the text gets too small). No vague benefits, each stage must state a specific, observable outcome. No non-sequential time jumps (the progression must feel logical).

## Typography notes

- **Time labels** are bold, slightly larger than benefit text. They anchor each stage visually. Color can match brand primary or progress from muted to saturated.
- **Benefit statements** are medium-weight, 3-8 words. Specific observable outcomes, not vague promises.
- **Connector line/dots** are subtle, they guide the eye downward but don't dominate.
- **Headline** at top: bold, large. Frames the timeline as a journey worth taking.
- **Font style:** Geometric sans-serif throughout. Bold for time labels and headline, medium for benefits.
- **Progressive visual treatment:** Colors, icon fills, or indicator sizes should subtly increase along the timeline, reinforcing the idea of cumulative benefit.

## Constraints

- **3-4 time stages maximum.** More creates tiny text and loses the clean visual flow.
- **Time progression must be logical.** Day 1 → Week 1 → Month 1 → Month 3 works. Random jumps (Day 1 → Year 1) do not.
- **Benefits must be specific and stage-appropriate.** Day 1 benefits should be immediate and sensory ("hydratation immédiate"). Month 3 benefits should be cumulative and transformative ("teint uniformisé").
- **Only for repeatable-use products.** The format makes no sense for one-time purchases.
- **No more than 8 words per benefit line.** Compression is essential for mobile readability.
- **Visual progression is mandatory.** Without it, the timeline is just a list. The warmth/saturation progression carries the narrative.

## Example use

MOFU brief for NaturAloé Gel Originel: Product at top-left. Timeline flowing down: Stage 1 "Jour 1" → "Hydratation immédiate, la peau boit le gel." Stage 2 "Semaine 1" → "Tiraillements et rougeurs s'estompent." Stage 3 "Mois 1" → "Peau souple, douce, visiblement apaisée." Stage 4 "Mois 3" → "Teint unifié, éclat naturel retrouvé." Progressive sage-green indicators from light to fully saturated. Headline: "Ce qui se passe quand vous passez au gel natif."
