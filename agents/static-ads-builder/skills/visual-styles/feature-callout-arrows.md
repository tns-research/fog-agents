# Feature Callout with Arrows

## Description

A product-centered static where the product photo occupies the center of the frame, with 2-4 thin annotation arrows or connector lines pointing from the product to short text labels that name key features, ingredients, or differentiators. The format reads like an exploded diagram or an editorial product breakdown, it turns the product itself into a visual argument for why it's better. Each arrow says: "look closer, here's what makes this special."

## Performance signal

Ingredient-focused callouts are top performers in skincare and beauty on Meta. The Body Shop's ginger shampoo ad (ingredient callouts: Madagascan ginger root, Mexican organic aloe vera, Birch bark) reached 1M views with 7 duplicates, a strong duplication signal of scaling (TrendTrack analysis). Curology runs lo-fi product photos with sticky-note-style callouts to great effect (Backlinko). The format works because it gives the viewer specific, verifiable reasons to trust the product, not vague claims, but named ingredients or features.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Avoid. Cold audiences don't know enough about the category to care about specific features.
- **MOFU:** Primary and ideal fit. The audience is comparing options and wants to know what's inside / what makes this different. Feature callouts answer that question visually.
- **BOFU:** Light use. Can work as a product-detail reminder for warm audiences, but BOFU needs offers and friction reduction more than education.

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed). Square 1:1 also works well for this format. 9:16 for Stories.
- **Camera position:** Frontal or slightly elevated (15-30°). The product must be clearly visible and label-facing for the arrows to point to meaningful areas.
- **Focal length:** 50-85mm. Sharp product rendering with slight background separation.
- **Lighting:** Even, bright, clean. Studio softbox or diffused window light. No dramatic shadows, every part of the product must be visible for the callouts to work.
- **Color grade:** Clean, bright, neutral. Minimal grading, the product and text must be perfectly legible.
- **Environment:** Clean background, solid color or subtle texture. The product floats visually; the arrows and text labels are the compositional elements.
- **Composition logic:**
  - **Product centered** in the frame, occupying 40-50% of the image area. Label visible, key features visible.
  - **2-4 annotation arrows** extending from specific points on the product to text labels placed around the product. Arrows are thin, clean lines (not thick or decorative), they serve as connectors, not visual elements.
  - **Text labels** are short (2-5 words each): ingredient names, percentages, certifications, or key feature names. Placed in clean zones around the product with enough spacing to avoid clutter.
  - **Arrow style options:** straight thin lines with dot endpoints, subtle curved connectors, or bracket-style annotations. Consistent style across all callouts.
  - Headline at top or bottom, framing the callouts: "Ce qu'il y a vraiment dedans." or "Zoom sur la composition."
- **Subject:** No human subject. Product-only.
- **Exclusions:** No more than 4 callout arrows (clutter threshold). No decorative arrows or thick graphic elements. No long text at callout points, 2-5 words each. No overlapping arrows. No callouts pointing to nothing specific on the product.

## Typography notes

- **Callout labels** are the key text elements, rendered inside the image as part of the composition. Short (2-5 words), bold, consistent size across all labels. Same font, same weight, same color.
- **Arrows** are connectors, not decorative elements. Thin, precise, consistent in style. They point from the label to a specific area of the product.
- **Headline** frames the callouts: "Ce qu'il y a dedans." / "Zoom sur la formule." Bold, large, at top or bottom.
- **Font style:** Clean geometric sans-serif. Medium weight for labels (not too heavy, they shouldn't compete with the product). Bold for headline.

## Constraints

- **2-4 callouts maximum.** More creates visual clutter and tiny text. Fewer is better if the claims are strong.
- **Each callout must point to a specific area of the product.** Random placement of arrows with no visual connection to the product breaks the editorial logic.
- **Callout text must be factual.** Named ingredients, percentages, certifications, or measurable features only. No vague adjectives ("amazing formula").
- **Arrows must not overlap each other or the product label.** Clean spatial separation is essential.
- **MOFU only as primary.** Cold audiences don't care about ingredients; warm audiences actively seek them.

## Example use

MOFU brief for NaturAloé Gel Originel: Product centered on white background. Three thin dark-sage arrows pointing to: (1) the gel visible through packaging → "94% pulpe fraîche d'aloé vera", (2) the Bio certification label → "Certifié Bio, Cosmos Organic", (3) the base of the tube → "Gel natif non reconstitué". Headline: "Lisez bien l'étiquette : il n'y a rien à cacher."
