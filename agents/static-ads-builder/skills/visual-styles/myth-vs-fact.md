# Myth vs. Fact

## Description

A two-zone static that presents a common consumer belief labeled "MYTHE" (or "MYTH") on one side, and the corrective truth labeled "FAIT" (or "FACT") on the other. The product appears alongside or below the fact zone as the embodiment of the truth. The format earns attention through cognitive dissonance, the viewer's existing belief is challenged, and the resolution (the fact + product) satisfies the tension.

## Performance signal

"People love myth-busting. It grabs attention and shifts beliefs fast" (Magritte/20K-ad analysis). Myth vs. Fact is identified as an "under the radar" format, used by few brands but consistently effective (Nutrition Geeks, Happy Mammoth, Stakt). Holo AI also identifies "Mythbuster Ads" as a top static format for skincare: "These ads grab attention because they challenge what people think they know. Educating them places you as a thought leader" (Holo 2026 guide).

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Strong fit. The myth-busting hook works on cold audiences because it challenges a widely-held belief, no prior product awareness needed. The format educates while positioning the brand as the authority.
- **MOFU:** Primary fit. Warm audiences comparing options benefit from having misconceptions corrected, it shifts the consideration criteria in the brand's favor.
- **BOFU:** Avoid. The educational framing is wrong for conversion. BOFU needs offers, not education.

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed). 9:16 for Stories. Square 1:1 acceptable.
- **Camera position:** N/A, this is a typography-and-layout-dominant format. Product shot (if present) is frontal, clean.
- **Focal length:** 50-85mm for product element.
- **Lighting:** Even, bright, flat. Clean studio for product element.
- **Color grade:** Clean, high-contrast. Two distinct color zones for myth and fact.
- **Environment:** Two color zones, no environmental context.
- **Composition logic:**
  - **Option A (top/bottom split):** Upper zone (40%): "MYTHE" label in bold red or muted color, followed by the myth statement in large text. Lower zone (60%): "FAIT" label in bold green or brand color, followed by the fact statement in large text. Product placed in the fact zone or at the bottom.
  - **Option B (left/right split):** Left zone (45%): myth with muted/red treatment. Right zone (55%): fact with bright/green/brand treatment + product.
  - **The myth zone must feel "wrong"**: muted colors, crossed-out or red-flagged, slightly uncomfortable.
  - **The fact zone must feel "right"**: bright, clean, authoritative, trustworthy. Product lives here.
  - The visual weight must favor the fact zone (larger, brighter, more saturated).
- **Subject:** No human subject. Typography-and-product forward.
- **Exclusions:** No ambiguous myths, the myth must be something the target audience actually believes. No long explanations, both myth and fact are 1-2 sentences max. No equal visual weight, the fact zone must dominate.

## Typography notes

- **"MYTHE" and "FAIT" labels** are the structural anchors. Bold, uppercase, color-coded (red/muted for myth, green/brand for fact). Large enough to scan instantly.
- **Myth statement** is the first text the viewer reads. It must state a belief they hold, recognition creates the cognitive hook.
- **Fact statement** is the resolution. It must directly contradict the myth with a specific, verifiable claim.
- **Font style:** Geometric sans-serif, bold throughout. Same font for both zones, the color and zone treatment carry the contrast, not the font.
- **Product** is positioned in the fact zone only, placing it in the myth zone would create the wrong association.

## Constraints

- **The myth must be something the target audience actually believes.** If it's not a real misconception, the format fails, there's no cognitive dissonance to resolve.
- **Both myth and fact are 1-2 sentences maximum.** The format's power is in compression and contrast, not explanation.
- **The fact must be specific and verifiable.** Vague facts ("our product is better") don't carry authority. Specific facts do ("94% d'aloé vera pur vs. 10% dans les gels classiques").
- **Visual weight must favor the fact zone.** The fact zone gets more space (55-60%), brighter colors, and the product.
- **No BOFU use.** The educational framing does not convert, it positions and informs.

## Example use

TOFU brief for NaturAloé Gel Originel: Top zone (muted warm grey): "MYTHE" in red. "Tous les gels d'aloé vera sont identiques." Bottom zone (clean white): "FAIT" in sage green. "La plupart sont reconstitués à partir de poudre. Le nôtre est pressé à froid à partir de pulpe fraîche, avec 94% d'aloé vera pur." Product lower-right in the fact zone.
