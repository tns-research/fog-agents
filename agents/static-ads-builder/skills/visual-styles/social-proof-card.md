# Social Proof Card

## Description

A static that centers a real customer quote, bold, highlighted, emotionally resonant, alongside a star rating and the product. The customer's words are the hero; the product is the supporting evidence. The format mimics the trust signal of reading a review, but compressed into a single scroll-stopping image. The quote is often highlighted (marker effect, colored background strip, or bold weight) to draw the eye to the most emotionally impactful phrase.

## Performance signal

Social proof statics are consistently effective across DTC categories on Meta. "Feels like a real person answering real questions, not a brand selling" (Magritte/20K analysis). ARMRA's "Goodbye Bloat" social proof card drove massive engagement through empathy-to-authority transition (Brick by Brick DTC analysis). AKT London combined social proof (11,000+ 5-star reviews) with offer framing to reach 48M impressions. Trust is the #1 barrier at MOFU, this format removes it.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Avoid as primary. Social proof requires the audience to care about the product category before a review feels relevant. Exception: if the review is so emotionally striking that it functions as a hook on its own.
- **MOFU:** Primary fit. Trust is the main barrier at consideration stage. A real customer voice saying "this changed my skin" carries more weight than any brand claim.
- **BOFU:** Strong secondary fit. For warm audiences near conversion, a testimonial card can be the final nudge, "someone like me already tried it and loved it."

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed). 9:16 for Stories.
- **Camera position:** Frontal, clean. Product is a supporting element, not the hero.
- **Focal length:** 50-85mm for product.
- **Lighting:** Even, bright studio. No drama, trust requires clarity, not mood.
- **Color grade:** Clean, warm-neutral. Brand primary as accent color for highlights and rating stars.
- **Environment:** Clean background, solid color or subtle gradient. No distracting context.
- **Composition logic:**
  - **Option A (quote-dominant):** Upper 60%: customer quote in large bold text on clean background. Key emotional phrase highlighted with marker effect or colored background strip. Star rating (★★★★★) above or below the quote. Customer first name and optional descriptor ("Marie, 34 ans" or "Cliente depuis 2 ans"). Lower 40%: product standing upright, label visible.
  - **Option B (customer-photo + quote):** Left 40%: customer photo (real, authentic, not polished). Right 60%: quote, stars, and product. The photo adds authenticity, the face makes the quote feel real.
  - **Option C (review screenshot style):** Full frame styled as a review screenshot, white card with grey border, star rating at top, review text below, product image at bottom. Feels native to review platforms.
  - The **highlighted phrase** is the visual anchor, the one sentence that makes the viewer think "I want that result."
- **Subject:** Customer photo optional (adds authenticity). Product required.
- **Exclusions:** No fake quotes. No stock photography for customer photos. No more than 3-4 sentences in the quote (keep it punchy). No brand claims mixed with customer quotes, the voice must be singular.

## Typography notes

- **Customer quote** is the dominant text element. Bold sans-serif, large enough to read as thumbnail. The highlighted phrase should be visually distinct, marker effect (yellow or brand color behind text), colored strip, or weight change.
- **Star rating** is a secondary visual anchor. Gold or brand-color stars. Positioned near the quote to reinforce credibility.
- **Customer attribution** is small, understated, first name and one descriptor. Not a full bio.
- **Font style:** Sans-serif, bold for quote, medium for attribution. The quote should feel like real speech, not marketing copy.
- **Headline (optional):** If present, sits above everything. Frames the social proof: "Ce que nos clientes en disent." or the highlighted result claim.

## Constraints

- **Quote must be real and attributable.** Do not invent testimonials. Use actual customer reviews, social media comments, or survey responses.
- **One voice only.** Do not mix brand copy with customer quotes in the same frame, the authenticity collapses if the voice shifts.
- **1-3 sentences maximum for the quote.** Longer quotes lose readability and the viewer moves on.
- **The highlighted phrase is the anchor.** If you remove the highlight, the quote should still work, but the highlight makes the key emotional claim unmissable.
- **Customer photo must be authentic** if included. No stock, no retouched studio portraits. iPhone selfie quality is ideal.

## Example use

MOFU brief for NaturAloé Gel Originel: Clean cream background. Large bold quote: "J'ai essayé des dizaines de gels. Celui-ci est le seul qui ne colle pas, ne tire pas, et sent juste la plante." Highlighted phrase: "le seul qui ne colle pas" with sage-green marker effect. ★★★★★ in gold. Attribution: "Sophie, 41 ans". Product standing below, centered. No headline, the quote is the headline.
