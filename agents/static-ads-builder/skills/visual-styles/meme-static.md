# Meme-Style Static

## Description

Problem-first typographic card where bold text is the primary visual, no product shown. Reads like organic social content rather than an ad. Works because it bypasses the "is this an ad?" recognition filter in cold audiences: the format looks like something a friend shared, not a paid placement.

## Performance signal

Highest scroll-stop rate in cold-audience prospecting for problem-aware messaging (Meta DTC data, 2025). The format's strength is its categorical disguise, it looks like content, not advertising. Engagement rates decline sharply if any element reads as polished or branded (logo strip, gradient background, product photo). The less it looks like an ad, the better it performs.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Exclusive fit. This is a discovery and problem-recognition format. The absence of a product is intentional, it opens a question the audience didn't know they had.
- **MOFU:** Do not use. A warm audience expects differentiation and proof, not a problem hook with no resolution. The format creates friction for someone already in consideration.
- **BOFU:** Do not use. No product, no offer, no CTA, wrong stage entirely.

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed). 9:16 for stories. Square 1:1 works well for this format.
- **Camera position:** Not applicable, no photography element. Frontal flat design.
- **Focal length:** Not applicable.
- **Lighting:** Studio overhead softbox, centered, flat diffuse, no shadows, or no lighting spec (pure flat design). The background must be even and texture-free.
- **Color grade:** Not applicable for photographic elements. Background is a flat matte color: off-white, light grey, near-black, or a single saturated brand color. No gradients unless very subtle. No texture.
- **Environment:** Not applicable. Background is a flat design surface only.
- **Composition logic:** Typography is the entire visual. Oversized bold headline dominates the upper two-thirds. Secondary line (if any) in smaller weight below. Optional: a single small icon, emoji-style element, or thin divider line, but text must dominate. No product. No face. No photography.
- **Subject:** None. This is a text-only creative.
- **Exclusions:** No product packaging. No face or person. No photography element. No marble or wood surface backgrounds. No busy graphic elements competing with the text. Never include a CTA ("Shop now", "Buy here"), the format's job is to stop the scroll and raise a question, not to convert.

## Typography notes

- **Weight:** Extra-bold or black weight for the primary line. The size should be large enough that the first 3-4 words are readable as a thumbnail.
- **Font style:** Modern sans-serif (geometric or humanist). No decorative or display fonts that signal "brand."
- **Color:** High contrast against background, white on dark, dark on light, or a single brand accent color used sparingly.
- **Placement:** Headline centered or left-aligned in the upper two-thirds. Do not push headline to the lower half, the eye needs to hit the text within the first 500ms of the scroll.
- **No CTA in the visual.** Never include a call-to-action button or "Shop now" text in the image, it immediately signals "ad" and breaks the organic-content illusion.

## Constraints

- **TOFU only.** Do not apply at MOFU or BOFU.
- **No product in frame.** Even a blurred or secondary product reference breaks the meme-content disguise.
- **No face or person.** Adding a subject shifts this toward a testimonial or lifestyle format.
- **No CTA text in image.** The hook is the entire job of this creative.
- Maximum **2 briefs per batch** using this style, overuse signals the brand has no real product to show.

## Example use

TOFU brief (Wrong Answer angle) for a skincare brand: off-white matte background, oversized bold dark headline "La carboxythérapie, c'est pour les médecins ?" in the upper half, secondary line "On a fait un masque. Pour tout le monde." in lighter weight below. Nothing else in the frame.
