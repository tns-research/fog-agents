# Mini Landing Page

## Description

A dense, information-rich static that functions as a compressed landing page in a single image. Multiple elements, product photo, headline, 2-3 benefits, a proof element (review snippet, certification badge, or stat), and a CTA, are arranged in a structured grid or vertical stack. The format works for audiences who need everything in one frame to make a decision. It's the "show me everything at once" format for high-intent viewers.

## Performance signal

"Ads that look like Pinterest boards function as a mini landing page in a single image", one of the underrated trends of 2025-2026 (Backlinko, How Deep Will You Go? example). Text-heavy statics work as "mini direct-response sales pages" when the message is complex (Backlinko, Ridge example). Curtis Howland identifies "text-heavy mini landing page style" as one of four foundational static formats: "Use for offer-driven campaigns." The format's density is its strength, it gives high-intent viewers everything they need to click.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Avoid. Cold audiences don't have the patience or motivation to process a dense layout. The format requires existing interest.
- **MOFU:** Strong fit. Warm audiences actively seeking information will engage with a dense layout that answers multiple questions at once. The format respects their decision-making process.
- **BOFU:** Primary fit. For audiences near conversion, the mini LP combines the product, the proof, the offer, and the CTA in one frame, removing the need to click through to learn more.

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed) is primary, the taller format gives more vertical stacking space. 9:16 for Stories (even more space). Avoid 1:1, too cramped for this density.
- **Camera position:** Frontal, clean for product element. The layout is graphic-design-driven, not photography-driven.
- **Focal length:** 50-85mm for product element.
- **Lighting:** Even, bright, studio for product. Clean and consistent.
- **Color grade:** Clean, brand-consistent. Sections can have subtle color zone differences (e.g., white zone for product, light brand-color zone for benefits, dark zone for CTA).
- **Environment:** No environmental context. The frame IS the environment, a designed layout with zones, cards, and sections.
- **Composition logic:**
  - **Structured vertical stack or grid layout.** The frame is divided into distinct visual zones, each containing one element:
    - **Zone 1 (top 15-20%):** Headline, bold, attention-grabbing, the hook.
    - **Zone 2 (20-30%):** Product photo, clean, label visible, hero placement.
    - **Zone 3 (20-25%):** Benefits, 2-3 short benefit lines with icons or checkmarks.
    - **Zone 4 (15-20%):** Proof, star rating, review snippet, certification badge, press mention, or stat.
    - **Zone 5 (bottom 10-15%):** CTA, bold button-style element or clear "Shop now" / "Découvrir" text.
  - Zones separated by spacing, subtle lines, or color shifts. The layout must feel organized, not chaotic.
  - **Visual hierarchy flows top-to-bottom:** hook → product → reasons → proof → action.
- **Subject:** No human subject. Product-and-information forward.
- **Exclusions:** No lifestyle imagery competing with the information density. No more than 5 zones (keep it structured, not chaotic). No tiny text, every element must be readable at mobile scale. No competing visual weights, the hierarchy must be clear.

## Typography notes

- **Headline** is the largest text element, it's the hook that earns further scanning. Extra-bold, top of frame.
- **Benefit text** is medium-bold, consistent size. 3-6 words each, with icons.
- **Proof text** is lighter weight, it supports, not leads. Star rating in gold/brand color. Review quote in italics if included.
- **CTA text** is bold, inside a button-shaped element. High contrast against button color. Action-oriented verb.
- **Font style:** Single typeface throughout (geometric sans-serif), with hierarchy created through weight (extra-bold → bold → medium → regular) and size.
- **The layout must flow top-to-bottom like a landing page.** The viewer's eye should naturally move from headline → product → benefits → proof → CTA.

## Constraints

- **Maximum 5 zones.** More zones create confusion; the format's power is in organized density, not chaos.
- **Every element must be readable at mobile thumbnail scale.** If you can't read the headline in a thumbnail, the text is too small.
- **Clear visual hierarchy.** The eye must know where to go first (headline), second (product), third (benefits), etc. If everything competes, nothing wins.
- **Not for TOFU.** The density overwhelms cold audiences. Reserve for warm/hot audiences who are ready to process information.
- **CTA is mandatory.** Unlike other formats where the CTA can be implicit, the mini LP format expects an explicit next step, it's modeled on a landing page, and landing pages have buttons.

## Example use

BOFU brief for NaturAloé Gel Originel: White background with subtle sage sections. Zone 1: "Le gel d'aloé vera que votre peau attendait." Zone 2: Product hero, centered. Zone 3: Three sage checkmarks, "94% aloé vera pur", "Certifié Bio", "Fabriqué en France". Zone 4: "★★★★★ Plus de 200 avis vérifiés". Zone 5: Sage-green rounded CTA button: "Découvrir le Gel Originel".
