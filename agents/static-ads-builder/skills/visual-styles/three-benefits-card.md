# Three Benefits Card

## Description

A clean, structured static where the product occupies one zone of the frame and three key benefits are listed alongside it, each with a short line of text and an optional icon or checkmark. The layout reads like a mini product page compressed into one scroll-stopping image. The benefits do the selling; the product provides visual proof.

## Performance signal

Listicle-style statics are among the highest-performing formats in DTC Meta Ads (2025-2026). "People love lists. It's easy to skim and super convincing when done right" (Magritte/20K-ad analysis). The format works because it hits multiple purchase motivations in a single frame, a viewer who doesn't care about benefit #1 might convert on benefit #3. Brands like Ovrload, Arrae, Flo, and Grüns run this format at scale. The icon + short line structure enables processing in under 2 seconds.

## Funnel fit

- **TOFU:** Valid. Use benefits that name the problem being solved (outcome-first, not feature-first). The listicle structure earns scroll-stop through visual rhythm, the eye wants to finish the list.
- **MOFU:** Primary fit. Warm audiences comparing options need to see a clear, scannable value proposition. This format answers "why this one?" without requiring effort.
- **BOFU:** Avoid as primary. At BOFU the audience already knows the benefits, they need friction reduction (price, offer, guarantee), not education.

## Visual recipe

- **Format + crop:** Vertical 4:5 (feed). 9:16 for Stories. Square 1:1 acceptable.
- **Camera position:** Frontal or slightly elevated (15-30°), clean and analytical. Product is static, not dynamic.
- **Focal length:** 50-85mm equivalent for product. Sharp, clean, no distortion.
- **Lighting:** Studio overhead softbox or diffused window light. Even, bright, no dramatic shadows. Product and background evenly lit for clean legibility.
- **Color grade:** Clean, bright, neutral digital. Brand primary color used as accent for icons, checkmarks, or benefit number badges. No heavy cinematic grade, readability is the priority.
- **Environment:** Clean background, solid color (white, cream, light brand color) or subtle gradient. No busy context competing with the benefit text.
- **Composition logic:**
  - **Option A (product-left / benefits-right):** Product occupies left 35-40% of frame, standing upright, label visible. Right 60-65%: three benefit lines stacked vertically, each with a checkmark, icon, or number badge on the left and a short benefit statement on the right. Headline at top spanning full width.
  - **Option B (product-top / benefits-below):** Product centered in upper 40% of frame. Three benefits listed below in a clean stack, separated by thin lines or spacing. Headline above or below product.
  - **Option C (product-center / benefits-around):** Product centered. Benefits placed around the product with subtle connector lines or arrows pointing from benefit text to relevant product area.
  - Benefit text must be **immediately scannable**: the viewer should grasp all three in under 2 seconds.
- **Subject:** No human subject required. Product-forward.
- **Exclusions:** No lifestyle context competing with the benefit list. No more than 3 benefits (adding more reduces scan speed). No long sentences, each benefit is 3-6 words maximum. No decorative elements that don't serve readability.

## Typography notes

- **Headline** sits at the top of the frame, spanning full width. Bold, large, attention-grabbing, it's the hook that earns the scan of the three benefits.
- **Benefit text** is rendered inside the image as part of the composition, described in `image_prompt`. Each line: bold sans-serif, 3-6 words, same size across all three.
- **Icons or checkmarks** are visual anchors for each benefit, same style, same color (brand primary), same size.
- **Font style:** Geometric sans-serif, bold or extra-bold for headline, medium for benefit lines. High legibility at mobile thumbnail size.

## Constraints

- **Maximum 3 benefits.** More dilutes scan speed and creates clutter. If the product has more than 3 selling points, pick the three that matter most for the funnel stage.
- **Benefit text must be 3-6 words per line.** Not sentences, compressed value statements. "94% pure aloé vera", "Certifié Bio", "Hydrate en 30 secondes".
- **Icons must be consistent**: same style, same size, same color. Mixed icon styles look unprofessional.
- **No human subject required.** This is an information-architecture format, not a lifestyle format.

## Example use

MOFU brief for a French skincare brand (NaturAloé Gel Originel): product standing on clean cream background, left 35%. Right side: three benefits with sage-green checkmarks, "94 % d'aloé vera pur", "Gel natif pressé à froid", "Convient à toute la famille". Headline at top: "Trois raisons de ne plus jamais acheter un gel en supermarché."
